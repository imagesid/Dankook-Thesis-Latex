\chapter{Method}

The conclusion generally consists of a single chapter and includes discussions of the research results, conclusions, and suggestions for future work.

\section{Discussion}

\textbf{Function of the Discussion}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item To provide answers to the research questions presented in the introduction
    \item To explain the results supporting the answers to the research questions and describe how these answers fit within the current body of knowledge
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Contents Included in the Discussion}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Answers to the research questions raised in the introduction (the first sentence of the discussion should restate the research question introduced earlier and immediately provide the answer)

    \item The discussion should include support, explanation, and defense of the answers; explanations for results that do not support the answers; the novelty of the study; differences from previous studies; explanations of unexpected results; limitations of the research methods or study design; validity of the research assumptions; and implications regarding the importance and significance of the study
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Verb Tense in the Discussion}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Verb tense should be selected according to whether the sentence describes the answer to the research question or the research results
    \item Present tense should be used when discussing answers to the research questions
    \item Past tense should be used when describing research results
\end{enumerate}
